Qualify an Inbound Lead with an AI Crew — Scored, Routed, and Honest About Guesses — Tutorial¶

In ~30 minutes, no code, you build a two-agent inbound-lead qualification crew, paste in a raw lead record, and watch it extract firmographics with per-field confidence, score ICP fit 0–100, and — the part that keeps your CRM clean — flag the fields it can't verify (and refuse to invent a website or a headcount) instead of guessing, then hand your rep a routing decision and a ready-to-use outreach angle, all in an auditable trace, deployed in your own workspace.

Step 1 — Describe what you want (Crew Studio)¶

A BDR's inbox is a firehose of inbound leads — contact-form submissions, enriched account notes, scribbled discovery-call notes. Each one has to be read, qualified, scored against your ideal customer profile (ICP), and either handed to an Account Executive, put on nurture, or dropped. The fastest way to poison a pipeline is a BDR who fills the blanks with guesses — a made-up headcount, an assumed website, an ICP score with nothing behind it. That judgment work, at volume and honestly, is exactly what a crew of AI agents is good at — if you can trust it. Let's build that crew, no code.

Open Crew Studio from the left rail (under Build). You'll land on "What would you like to build today?" with a Describe your automation box. Paste this in and hit the blue → (Build automation):

Qualify an inbound B2B sales lead. Given the pasted text of a lead or company record (a contact-form submission, an enriched account note, or notes from a discovery call), first have a BDR research analyst extract firmographics (company name, industry, employee count, region, website) and intent signals, and score ICP fit from 0 to 100, assigning a confidence level to each extracted field, never inventing firmographic facts the text does not support. Then have a sales-manager reviewer validate the ICP score, flag any weak or assumed signals and any disqualifiers, recommend the next action (route to an Account Executive / nurture / disqualify) with reasoning, and draft a short personalized outreach angle for the rep.

💡 What just happened. You didn't pick a template — you described the job in plain English. Studio reads that and proposes a crew: a team of AI agents, each with a role, that work in sequence. That's the core CrewAI idea, and you'll watch it take shape.

✅ Checkpoint: Studio opens a project editor with a Studio Chat panel and a Canvas.

Step 2 — Steer the build (keep it self-contained — and tool-free)¶

Studio now works out how to build your crew. It may do one of two things — either ask a couple of clarifying questions first, or go straight to proposing a plan.

If it asks questions, answer with the self-contained option each time: - Where the lead comes from → Plain text input (manually pasted) (not a CRM, form tool, or inbox). - Where the output should go → Display in the crew output only (not a Sheet, Slack, or CRM record).

Then read the plan carefully — one thing usually needs fixing here. Because this is a sales research job, Studio often tries to be helpful and attaches web-search / website-scraping tools to the analyst (you'll see something like ExaSearchTool and ScrapeWebsiteTool listed under its Tools). For a self-contained build that's exactly what you don't want — those tools need external API keys, and more importantly they let the agent go fetch data instead of being honest about what your lead record actually contains.

So steer it back. Click Needs changes (below the plan) and send a message like:

Remove ExaSearchTool and ScrapeWebsiteTool from the BDR Research Analyst so both agents have Tools: None. Reason only over the pasted lead_text — do not scrape or search any website. Mark any field not present in the text as Unverifiable / Low confidence.

Studio will regenerate the plan. Confirm the analyst now shows Tools: None before you move on. (On our build it did exactly this on the first revision.)

💡 Why this matters. This is the whole honesty story in miniature. A lead-qualifier that can secretly scrape the web will always find something to put in the box — and you'll never know which fields were in the lead and which the AI went and guessed. Force it to reason over what your form actually captured, and its "I can't verify this" becomes information you can trust.

✅ Checkpoint: Studio shows a plan; you've steered it to no external tools (both agents Tools: None).

Step 3 — Confirm the plan → your crew appears¶

Read the (revised) plan Studio proposes — you'll recognize your two agents, both now with Tools: None, plus a single input variable {lead_text} — then click Confirm plan. (Nothing is built until you confirm; if you tried to Run now you'd get "No tasks found.")

Watch the Canvas build in front of you:

  • a BDR research analyst — reads the raw lead and extracts firmographics (company, industry, size, region, website) and intent signals, puts a confidence level on each field, lists what's missing or unverifiable, and scores ICP fit 0–100 with a written rationale. (Studio names it something like BDR Research Analyst & ICP Scoring Specialist — the exact wording varies.)
  • a sales-manager reviewer — a second agent that QAs the analyst's score, flags weak or assumed signals and any disqualifiers, makes the routing call (Route to AE / Nurture / Disqualify), and drafts a personalized outreach angle. (Named something like Senior Sales Manager & Lead Routing Strategist.)

They run sequentially: the analyst scores, then the manager validates and routes. (A suggestion tip may appear in the chat panel — leave it open; it often holds a Run Automation button, so dismissing it just hides that button.) Studio also picks a project name on its own (e.g. B2B Lead Qualifier) — that varies run to run and doesn't matter; what matters is the two agents wired analyst → manager.

💡 The idea worth keeping. You didn't ask for a second opinion — but good sales process always has one: the BDR who scores a lead isn't the one who decides an AE should spend an hour on it. Notice AMP gave your crew that same separation — a manager agent that reviews the score and can revise it before anything is routed. That's why you can trust the recommendation later.

✅ Checkpoint: The Canvas shows two agents wired in order — a BDR research analyst → a sales-manager reviewer (Studio's auto-generated project name varies).

Step 4 — Run it on an inbound lead¶

Start a run by clicking the blue Run button (top right) — that reliably opens the run form. (You may also see a Run Automation button inside the Studio Chat panel; it does the same thing. Don't switch to the "Run" tab expecting a button — it can just show a "waiting for events…" spinner.) Because you chose plain-text input, there's nothing to connect — AMP gives you a simple form (titled Run parameters) with one box per input the plan generated; each box is labeled with the raw field name (on our build there was a single field, lead_text).

Paste in the lead below. It's a deliberately realistic-but-messy inbound lead — real buying intent, but with the gaps a form and a quick call usually leave behind:

INBOUND LEAD — website contact-form submission + BDR discovery-call notes

Contact form: Name — Dana Rourke. Work email — dana.rourke@gmail.com (a personal Gmail, not a company domain). Company (free-text) — "Fjordline Outfitters". Message — "Our support team's phone setup keeps dropping calls during peak season and we're losing customers. We want to switch before the holiday rush. Needs to work across our regional stores."

BDR notes (~6-min call): Dana described it as "outdoor-gear retail." Size felt mid-market — "a bunch of stores, maybe a few hundred people total? I'm honestly not sure of the exact count." Locations "across the Pacific Northwest, and opening in Colorado now" — no HQ address given. Couldn't confirm a company website — "marketing handles that, I don't have the URL handy." Strong urgency: wants a decision "in the next few weeks, definitely before Black Friday." Budget: not discussed. Authority: Dana said "I'm on the support team" — didn't confirm whether they own the buying decision. Offhand: "we trialed one of those VoIP startups but it didn't cover our in-store desk phones."

Your form may not match this exactly. Studio designs the inputs from your description, so the field name/count can vary run to run. If you see a single lead_text box, paste the whole lead in. If your build split it into several fields, map the lead across the boxes you see.

Fill every box the form shows, then click Execute.

💡 This is "your own data." Swap in a clean, strong-fit lead — a work email on a company domain, a confirmed headcount, a named VP of Support as the buyer — and re-run. Watch the ICP score climb and the routing flip toward Route to AE. The crew reasons about what you gave it, not a canned script.

✅ Checkpoint: The Run/execution view shows Execution started and a live Timeline.

Step 5 — Watch it think — scored, routed, and honest about guesses¶

This is the part a slide deck can't fake. The Timeline shows each agent as it runs — each one makes an LLM call (the AI model doing the actual reasoning), and you can watch it happen:

  • BDR Research Analyst → Extract Firmographics and Score ICP Fit: Started → LLM call (~6s) → Completed
  • Sales Manager Reviewer → Validate ICP Score and Recommend Next Action: Started → LLM call (~5s) → Completed

Click any step to open Event details (with a Raw Data tab) — the exact instruction the agent got and what it produced. Click the sales-manager's Completed step — then scroll past the Description (the agent's instructions) to the Output section below, which is the actual final result. On a real run of the Fjordline lead above it returned a Sales Manager Review with:

ICP Score: 70 (validated, not changed) — "strong intent signals… offset by uncertainty on budget, decision-making authority, and unverifiable firmographics."

Flagged Weak Signals: Employee Count ("maybe a few hundred", Medium confidence); Company Website — Unverifiable / Low confidence; Authority in Purchasing Decision — unclear who can actually buy.

Flagged Disqualifiers: None — relevant segment (outdoor-gear retail), good ICP fit.

Recommended Next Action: Nurture — strong intent and a real pain point, but budget and authority aren't confirmed, so nurture with proactive outreach before the Black Friday deadline rather than burning an AE slot yet. Plus a ready-to-use outreach angle referencing the exact pain, the deadline, and the prior VoIP trial.

Look closely at that Company Website line. The lead never gave a URL — so the crew marked it Unverifiable. It did not quietly invent fjordlineoutfitters.com to fill the box.

💡 Why this is the whole point. You can see which agent decided what, on which evidence, and how long it took — and you can see it flag what it couldn't verify rather than guess. When your sales lead asks "why is this a 70 and not a 90, and why nurture instead of AE?", the answer isn't "the AI said so" — it's a trace you can open, with the weak signals and the reasoning laid out. A BDR that tells you what it doesn't know is the one whose scores you can actually route on.

✅ Checkpoint: You've read the ICP score, the routing recommendation, and seen an unverifiable field flagged rather than invented in the trace.

Step 6 — Deploy it as a real service¶

A crew that only runs in the editor is a prototype. Make it real: click Deploy (top right). In Ready to go live, keep the suggested name (whatever Studio generated, e.g. B2B Lead Qualifier-v1), optionally add a release note, and click Deploy.

Go to Automations in the left rail — your crew is there, Source Studio. It first shows Deploy Enqueued / Building Images for Crew…, then goes Online, with its own endpoint (a web address other systems can call) and an access token. This can take a few minutes — if it looks stuck on Building Images for Crew, refresh the page and it'll flip to Online. Identify the one you just created (it's the newest entry — don't confuse it with an older, similarly-named crew someone made before). It now runs without your editor open — a service you (or the CRM/form tool you connect later) can call.

💡 What you just crossed. From "I built something in a tool" to "I have a running service in my workspace." Same crew, now deployed — and every lead you run against it shows up in the same kind of trace you just read.

What you did: in ~30 minutes, no code — you described a lead-qualification crew, steered it to stay self-contained and honest (no scraping), built it (a research analyst + a sales-manager reviewer), ran it on a messy inbound lead you chose, read the trace to see exactly how it scored and routed — including watching it flag what it couldn't verify instead of inventing it — and deployed it as a live service in your own workspace. Every claim it made, you could check.

Take it further: feed it a clean, strong-fit lead and watch the ICP score climb and the routing flip to Route to AE · paste an obvious mismatch and watch it Disqualify with reasons · deploy a second version and compare · swap the scenario for your own domain (the same shape works for claims triage, fraud triage, or consumer-complaint triage).

✅ Checkpoint: Your newly-deployed crew (the newest Studio-source entry) is listed under Automations and goes Online.
